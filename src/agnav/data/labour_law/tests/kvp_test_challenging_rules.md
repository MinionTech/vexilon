# KVP Test (Challenging Rules)
**Keywords:** kvp, policy, rule, unreasonable, inconsistent, social media policy, conduct policy, management directive, rule breach

## Overview
The "KVP Test" (from the KVP Co. Ltd. (1965) case) is the primary legal standard for determining if an employer's rule or policy is valid and enforceable against an employee where it is not specifically mentioned in the collective agreement.

## Mandatory Audit Criteria
Management's rule must satisfy ALL six of the following criteria to be valid:

1. **CA Consistency:** The rule must not be inconsistent with the collective agreement.
2. **Reasonableness:** The rule must not be unreasonable.
3. **Clear and Unequivocal:** The rule must be clear and unequivocal.
4. **Member Awareness:** The rule must be brought to the attention of the employee affected before the company can act on it.
5. **Notice of Consequences:** The employee must have been notified that a breach of such rule could result in his discharge or discipline.
6. **Consistent Enforcement:** Such rule should have been consistently enforced by the company from the time it was introduced.

## Strategic Defense Guidance
* If ANY of the six factors are not met, the discipline may be nullified.
* ENFORCEMENT GAP: If management has known about breaches by other employees but didn't discipline them, they have "waived" their right to enforce the rule strictly.
* REASONABLENESS: A rule that governs off-duty conduct or impacts personal privacy is often considered "unreasonable" unless a strong nexus exists.
