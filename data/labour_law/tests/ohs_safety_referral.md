# OHS Quick-Check & Referral
**Keywords:** unsafe work, refusal to work, safety hazard, ohs, wcb, worksafebc, dangerous work, safety violation, injury, hazard, dangerous, refuse work

## Overview
Occupational Health & Safety (OHS) issues involve the physical safety of members and are often governed by provincial legislation (e.g., the Workers Compensation Act and OHS Regulation) in addition to the collective agreement (e.g., Article 22).

## Mandatory Referral Criteria
If a member mentions unsafe work, a refusal to perform work due to safety concerns, or a work-related injury, YOU MUST immediately advise:

1. **OHS Disclaimer:** "This may be an Occupational Health & Safety (OHS) matter — please contact the Joint Occupational Health & Safety (JOHS) Committee immediately."
2. **Right to Refuse:** Advise the member of their legal right to refuse unsafe work under Section 3.12 of the OHS Regulation.
3. **Contact Info:** Provide the WorkSafeBC Prevention Line: 1-888-621-7233.
4. **Member Presence:** Remind the member that they have the right to have a steward or a JOHS committee representative present if they are being interviewed about a safety concern.

## Strategic Defense Guidance
* SAFETY OVER DISCIPLINE: Ensure the employer is not using discipline to retaliate against a member for exercising their right to refuse unsafe work.
* ARTICLE 22 COMPLIANCE: Most agreements have specific timelines for reporting and investigating safety incidents—consult the agreement directly for these.
