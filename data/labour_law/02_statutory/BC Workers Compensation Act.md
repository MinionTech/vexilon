# BC Workers Compensation Act
**Source:** [BC Laws - Workers Compensation Act](https://www.bclaws.gov.bc.ca/civix/document/id/complete/statreg/1996492_01)
**Part:** Part 2 - Occupational Health and Safety

## Section 21: General duties of employers
(1) Every employer must
(a) ensure the health and safety of
(i) all workers working for that employer, and
(ii) any other workers present at a workplace where that employer's work is being carried out, and
(b) comply with this Part, the regulations and any applicable orders.

(2) Without limiting subsection (1), an employer must
(a) remedy any workplace conditions that are hazardous to the health or safety of the employer's workers,
(b) ensure that the employer's workers
(i) are made aware of all known or reasonably foreseeable health or safety hazards to which they are likely to be exposed by their work,
(ii) are made aware of their rights and duties under this Part and the regulations, and
(iii) are provided with the information, instruction, training and supervision necessary to ensure the health and safety of those workers in carrying out their work and to ensure the health and safety of other workers at the workplace,
(c) establish occupational health and safety policies and programs in accordance with the regulations,
(d) provide and maintain in good condition protective equipment, devices and clothing as required by the regulations and ensure that these are used by the employer's workers,
(e) provide to the employer's workers the first aid equipment and services required by the regulations, and
(f) cooperate with the board, officers of the board and any other person carrying out a duty under this Part or the regulations.

## Section 47: Prohibited action
(1) An employer or supervisor must not take or threaten to take prohibited action against a worker because the worker
(a) has exercised a right or carried out a duty in accordance with this Part or the regulations,
(b) has testified or is about to testify in any proceeding, or
(c) has given any information to the board or an officer.

(2) Prohibited action includes any act or omission by an employer or supervisor that adversely affects the worker's terms or conditions of employment, including, without limitation,
(a) discharge, suspension, layoff or demotion,
(b) a change in the location of work,
(c) reduction in wages, or
(d) a change in the working hours.
