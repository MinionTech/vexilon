# Millhaven Nexus Audit (Off-Duty Conduct)

## Overview
The "Nexus Test" (from the Millhaven Fibres Ltd. case) is the primary legal standard for determining if an employer can discipline or discharge an employee for conduct occurring off-duty. Management must prove a "real and material connection" between the conduct and the employment relationship.

## Mandatory Audit Criteria
To justify discipline, management must prove at least ONE of these five factors:

1. **Reputational Harm:** The conduct harms (or has the potential to harm) the employer's reputation or product.
2. **Inability to Perform Duties:** The conduct renders the employee unable to perform their job satisfactorily (e.g., loss of required license).
3. **Impact on Co-workers:** The behaviour leads to other employees' refusal, reluctance, or inability to work with the member.
4. **Criminal Breach:** A serious breach of the Criminal Code injurious to the general reputation of the employer and its employees.
5. **Operational Interference:** The conduct creates difficulty for the employer in efficiently managing its business and directing its workforce.

## Strategic Defense Guidance
* MANAGEMENT BEARS THE BURDEN: They cannot rely on speculation or "embarrassment"—they must prove actual harm or substantial risk.
* ARTICLE 8.2 COMPLIANCE: Regardless of off-duty status, management must adhere to all disciplinary representation requirements.
