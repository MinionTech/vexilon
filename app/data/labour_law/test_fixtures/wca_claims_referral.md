# WCA Claims & Benefits Referral
**Keywords:** WCA, Workers Compensation, injury at work, disability, vocational rehab, WCB claim, worksafe claim, back injury, repetitive strain, carpal tunnel, mental disorder, chronic stress, medical review, adjudication

## Overview
Workers Compensation Act (WCA) matters involve claims for compensation, healthcare, and rehabilitation following a work-related injury or occupational disease. These are handled by WorkSafeBC (the Board) and are subject to specific statutory timelines and evidence requirements under Parts 4, 5, and 6 of the Act.

## Mandatory Referral Criteria
If a member mentions a work-related injury, a filed WCB claim, or a dispute over compensation/benefits, YOU MUST immediately advise:

1. **Claims Disclaimer:** "This involves a Workers Compensation (WCB) claim. While we can discuss the collective agreement aspects (like sick leave), the adjudication of your claim is governed by the Workers Compensation Act."
2. **Duty to Report:** Remind the member that they must report any injury to their employer immediately and file a 'Form 6' with WorkSafeBC.
3. **Review Division:** If a decision has been made by the Board that the member disagrees with, they have **90 days** to file a Request for Review with the Review Division.
4. **Appeals (WCAT):** Decisions from the Review Division can further be appealed to the Workers' Compensation Appeal Tribunal (WCAT) within **30 days**.

## Strategic Defense Guidance
* WORK REINTEGRATION: Monitor if the employer is following the "Duty to Accommodate" and "Duty to Cooperate" in return-to-work scenarios (WCA Part 4, Division 6).
* SICK LEAVE VS WCB: Ensure the member is not being forced to use sick bank for a clear workplace injury before a WCB claim is adjudicated.
