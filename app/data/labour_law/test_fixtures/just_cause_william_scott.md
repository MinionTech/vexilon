# Just Cause and the William Scott Test
**Keywords:** just cause, discipline, discharge, dismissal, termination, william scott, burden of proof, onus, three questions, mitigating factors, excessive

## Overview
The William Scott decision is the leading case for discipline and discharge in British Columbia. It establishes a three-question framework that arbitrators use to determine if discipline is justified and if the penalty is appropriate.

## Mandatory Logic Check: William Scott Test
You MUST evaluate the facts against these three questions in sequence:

### 1. Has the employee given just cause for some form of discipline?
* **Burden of Proof:** The employer must prove "on a balance of probabilities" that the worker did what they were accused of doing.
* **Onus:** The onus is entirely on the employer. If they cannot prove the facts, the discipline is not warranted.

### 2. If so, was the employer's decision to dismiss (or discipline) the employee an excessive response in all of the circumstances of the case?
* **Proportionality:** Even if the employee did the act, the punishment must fit the offense.
* **Mitigating Factors:** You MUST consider extenuating circumstances such as:
    - Length of service and previous good record.
    - Whether the incident was an isolated lapse or a pattern of conduct.
    - Whether the employee was provoked.
    - Whether the offense was committed under stress or impulsively.
    - Whether the employer has consistently enforced the rule in the past.

### 3. If the discharge/discipline was excessive, what alternative measure should be substituted as just and equitable?
* **Remedy:** Identify a more appropriate penalty (e.g., a shorter suspension or a written warning) based on the seriousness of the offense and the mitigating factors.

## Strategic Guidance
* **EXPLAIN:** Start by stating that the William Scott test applies.
* **QUESTION:** State which of the three questions is currently at issue.
* **APPLY:** Analyze the facts of the case (e.g., is there proof? are there mitigating factors?).
