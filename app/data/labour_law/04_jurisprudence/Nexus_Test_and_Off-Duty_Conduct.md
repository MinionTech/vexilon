# Nexus Test and Off-Duty Conduct: A Guide for Union Stewards

**Authority:** *Re Millhaven Fibres Ltd. & Oil, Chemical and Atomic Workers I.U. Loc. 9-670 (1967) (Ont. L.A.B.)*

## 1. Overview of the "Nexus Test"

The "Nexus Test" is the primary Canadian legal standard for determining if an employer can discipline or discharge an employee for conduct occurring off-duty. Management must prove a "real and material connection" (a nexus) between the off-duty conduct and the employment relationship to justify disciplinary action.

## 2. The Five Millhaven Factors (Criteria)

To justify discipline for off-duty conduct, management must demonstrate that the employee's behavior satisfies at least one of the following five criteria:

1. **Reputational Harm:** The conduct harms (or has the potential to harm) the employer's reputation or product (e.g., public scandal or damage to the "brand").
2. **Inability to Perform Duties:** The conduct renders the employee unable to perform their job satisfactorily (e.g., losing a required professional license or security clearance).
3. **Impact on Co-workers:** The behaviour leads to other employees' refusal, reluctance, or inability to work with the employee (e.g., harassment or threats made off-site to a colleague).
4. **Criminal Breach:** The employee has been guilty of a serious breach of the *Criminal Code*, rendering their conduct injurious to the general reputation of the employer and its employees.
5. **Operational Interference:** The conduct creates difficulty for the employer in properly carrying out its function of efficiently managing its business and directing its workforce.

## 3. Strategic Defense for Union Stewards (Procedural)

- **The Burden of Proof:** Management bears the burden of proving the nexus. They cannot rely on speculation or "embarrassment"—they must demonstrate actual harm or substantial risk.
- **Article 8.2 (Disciplinary Procedure):** Regardless of whether the conduct was off-set, management must strictly adhere to all disciplinary notice and representation requirements in the Collective Agreement.
- **Case-by-Case Context:** The level of trust and visibility of the role (e.g., public service) impacts the strength of the nexus required.

## 4. Operational Script: Meeting Questions for Management

- "Which of the five Millhaven factors is management specifically relying upon for this proposed discipline?"
- "Can management provide evidence of actual harm to the employer's reputation that has occurred as a result of this off-duty incident?"
- "How exactly does this incident prevent the member from fulfilling the specific duties of their [Job Title] role tomorrow?"
